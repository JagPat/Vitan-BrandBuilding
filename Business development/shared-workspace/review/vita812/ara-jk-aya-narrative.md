# JK Architect of the Year (AYA) 2026: Commendation Award (Public Building)
## Project: Ahmedabad Racquet Academy (ARA)

**Location:** Thaltej, Ahmedabad, Gujarat, India
**Category:** Commendation Award - Public Building
**Status:** Completed

---

### 1. Project Overview
The Ahmedabad Racquet Academy (ARA) is Gujarat’s first professional-grade multi-sport facility, spanning a 5-acre site. It represents a new paradigm for sports infrastructure in Western India, combining the rigors of professional training with a high-performance, climate-responsive architectural language.

### 2. Design Intent & Philosophy
The architectural approach is rooted in "purity of purpose." Deriving aesthetic value from structural honesty, the project utilizes exposed concrete as its primary palette. This choice provides the necessary thermal mass to regulate indoor temperatures in Ahmedabad’s extreme climate while lending the campus a timeless, institutional dignity.

### 3. Spatial Organization and Climate Response
The campus is meticulously zoned to balance diverse sport requirements. The outdoor facilities include a FIFA-approved artificial turf ground and seven synthetic tennis courts. These are connected to a high-volume indoor block housing badminton and squash courts. 

To counter the semi-arid climate, the design employs:
- **Deep-set Fenestrations:** Providing natural solar shading.
- **Strategically Positioned Screens:** Creating a dynamic play of light and shadow while facilitating natural air circulation.
- **Thermal Mass:** Using exposed concrete to minimize reliance on artificial cooling in transitional social zones.

### 4. Community and Regional Impact
Beyond its technical specifications, ARA has catalyzed the growth of professional sports in the region. By providing world-class infrastructure to local talent, it encourages a culture of "observational learning," where amateur enthusiasts can watch and learn from professional athletes. ARA stands as a landmark of functional minimalism that adds significant value to Ahmedabad’s urban fabric.
