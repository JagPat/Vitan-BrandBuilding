# WAF 2026 Submission Draft: Ahmedabad Racquet Academy (ARA)

**Project Name:** Ahmedabad Racquet Academy (ARA)
**Location:** Ahmedabad, Gujarat, India
**Category:** Public / Sports
**Status:** Completed

---

## 1. Project Summary (485 words)

The Ahmedabad Racquet Academy (ARA) is a pioneering institutional project that redefines sports infrastructure in Western India. Spanning a 5-acre site in the Thaltej precinct of Ahmedabad, the academy is Gujarat’s first professional-grade multi-sport facility. It was conceived not merely as a collection of courts and grounds, but as a "sanctuary of athleticism"—a space where the rigors of professional training meet the openness of community engagement.

### Design Intent & Philosophy
The architectural approach by Vitan Architects is rooted in "purity of purpose." In a climate where summer temperatures frequently exceed 45°C, the design challenge was to create a high-performance environment that remained energy-efficient and thermally comfortable. The philosophy rejects "false decorations," instead deriving aesthetic value from structural honesty and material truth. Exposed concrete serves as the primary palette, providing the necessary thermal mass to regulate temperature while lending the campus a timeless, institutional dignity.

### Spatial Organization & Context
The campus is meticulously zoned to balance the diverse requirements of indoor and outdoor sports. The outdoor facilities feature a FIFA-approved artificial turf ground—home to Gujarat's first professional football club, ARA FC—and seven synthetic tennis courts designed to international competition standards. These expansive open spaces are connected to the indoor volume via transition zones that serve as social hubs for athletes and visitors.

The indoor block houses four badminton courts in a high-volume hall, designed with a focus on shadowless lighting and natural air circulation. Two professional glass-back squash courts and a dedicated table tennis zone complete the racquet sports offering. Ancillary spaces, including a specialized gym, administrative blocks, and athlete recovery areas, are integrated seamlessly, ensuring a friction-less operational flow.

### Climate Responsiveness & Sustainability
Ahmedabad’s hot and dry climate dictated a robust environmental strategy. The buildings employ deep-set windows and strategically positioned screens to provide natural solar shading without compromising on ventilation. These elements create a play of light and shadow that changes throughout the day, adding a dynamic visual layer to the minimalist concrete surfaces. The choice of materials and the orientation of the blocks minimize the reliance on artificial cooling in transitional spaces, significantly reducing the facility's carbon footprint.

### Social and Community Impact
Beyond its technical specifications, ARA has had a profound impact on the regional sports ecosystem. By providing world-class infrastructure to local talent, it has catalyzed the growth of professional football and racquet sports in Gujarat. The design encourages a culture of "observational learning," where aspiring youth can watch professional athletes train, effectively blurring the lines between amateur enthusiasm and professional excellence.

In conclusion, the Ahmedabad Racquet Academy stands as a testament to the power of functional minimalism. It proves that institutional architecture can be both high-performing and emotionally resonant, serving as a landmark that "adds life" to the city’s urban fabric.
