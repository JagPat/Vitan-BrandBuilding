## 1. ARCASIA Forum 23 (New Delhi/Jaipur)
- **Dates**: Sep 8-12, 2026
- **Theme**: "Bringing Back… Vernacular Wisdom"
- **Estimated Deadline**: May 31, 2026 (CFP)
- **Portal**: [arcasiaforum23.com](https://arcasiaforum23.com)

### Abstract Draft: "The Math of Vernacular Wisdom: Scaling Passivity in High-Density Urbanism"
**Core Thesis**: Vernacular architecture is often romanticized as a low-rise, artisanal practice. This talk argues that the "wisdom" of the vernacular—passive cooling, material honesty, and climatic response—can and must be scaled for the high-density vertical cities of tomorrow. Using the **"Math of Beauty"** model, Jagrut Patel demonstrates how traditional Gujarat-inspired cross-ventilation and shading principles were translated into modern high-rise projects like **Seventy** and **Privilon**, delivering measurable ROI and human-centric comfort without relying on high-energy mechanical systems.

**Key Learning Points**:
- Reinterpreting traditional massing for wind-load and solar-gain optimization.
- The financial model for climate-responsive envelopes in commercial developments.
- Stewardship: Why "adding life" means protecting ancestral architectural logic.

## 2. Design Democracy (Hyderabad)
- **Dates**: Sep 18-20, 2026
- **Venue**: HITEX Exhibition Centre, Hyderabad
- **Theme Focus**: "Ahmedabad Roots" (Special Region Focus)
- **Status**: Curated/Invitation or Inquiry Based
- **Contact**: partnerships@designdemocracy.in / Instagram @designdemocracy.in

### Abstract Draft: "Ahmedabad Roots: The Execution Specialist as a Cultural Guardian"
**Core Thesis**: Ahmedabad is a city of architectural giants and deep modernist roots. For a practice to thrive here for 35 years, it must act as a bridge between that legacy and the relentless pace of modern delivery. Jagrut Patel explores the "Ahmedabad Roots" of Vitan Architects—not just in form, but in the **Efficiency Mandate**. This session showcases how the "Execution Specialist" role ensures that the high design standards of the Ahmedabad school survive the reality of the construction site, becoming profitable landmarks rather than compromised brochures.

**Key Learning Points**:
- The "Vitan Way": Translating Ahmedabad’s modernist legacy into 162m landmarks.
- Why viability is the best guardian of architectural identity.
- Case Study: Safal Vihaan — Stewardship of 500+ chikoo trees as a design driver.
