# Speaking Proposal: ZAK World of Facades 2026

**Topic:** The Performance of Texture: Climate-Responsive Envelopes in High-Density Indian Housing
**Project Case Study:** Augusta, Ahmedabad
**Speaker:** Ar. Jagrut Patel, Principal Architect, Vitan Architects

---

## 1. Abstract (300 words)

As Indian cities rapidly densify, the high-rise residential typology has become the default solution for urban housing. However, many contemporary developments rely on glass-heavy envelopes or flat, unshaded facades that exacerbate the urban heat island effect and increase cooling loads. 

In this session, Ar. Jagrut Patel presents a counter-approach through the lens of **Augusta**, a 170,000 sq. ft. residential development in Ahmedabad. The presentation focuses on how a "performance-first" facade strategy can transform a large-scale housing project from a monolithic block into a breathable, climate-responsive ecosystem.

Key technical themes include:
*   **The Vertical Fin as a Solar Filter:** Utilizing vertical fins not just as aesthetic markers, but as precise solar shading devices that reduce incident heat gain while maintaining daylighting quality.
*   **Deep Recessed Balconies:** The use of structural depth to create a "secondary skin," providing a transitional microclimate between the exterior and interior.
*   **Material Restraint vs. Environmental Durability:** Balancing the material palette to ensure long-term performance in Ahmedabad's harsh, semi-arid climate.
*   **The Economics of Facade Performance:** How optimizing the envelope can lead to significant execution savings and reduced operational costs for residents.

The talk aims to demonstrate that for the Indian context, "high-performance" doesn't necessarily mean high-cost or high-tech; it means high-sensitivity to local climatic realities.

## 2. Key Learning Objectives
1.  Understand how to utilize verticality and depth in facade design to mitigate solar gain in hot-dry climates.
2.  Learn strategies for scaling high-performance envelope design to large-scale, cost-sensitive residential projects.
3.  Analyze the relationship between facade texture, urban ventilation, and resident comfort.

## 3. Speaker Bio
Ar. Jagrut Patel is the founder and Principal Architect of Vitan Architects, Ahmedabad. With over three decades of experience, he has led the design of iconic commercial, residential, and institutional projects across Gujarat. His philosophy of "Adding life, every square foot" is rooted in the belief that architecture must be humane, contextually rooted, and economically disciplined. Jagrut is a frequent speaker at architecture forums, advocating for design that balances professional rigor with a deep sense of social responsibility.
