# SOCIAL_STRATEGY

## Last Updated
- 2026-04-03 (VITA-134 baseline update)

## Objective
Use social distribution as a conversion engine for client acquisition, not vanity publishing.

## Content Pillars (updated weights)
- 40% Project proof (before/after, constraints solved, measurable outcomes)
- 25% Founder POV (design philosophy translated to business/client value)
- 20% Process credibility (planning rigor, maintenance logic, lifecycle thinking)
- 15% Social proof (client outcomes, collaborator signals, awards/mentions when verified)

## Platform Execution Rules
- LinkedIn: primary authority platform; minimum 3 posts/week with one clear CTA each.
- Instagram: visual proof + concise narrative; maintain canonical brand identity in captions and bio.
- Facebook: repurpose LinkedIn narratives with localized CTA and simplified caption structure.
- X: use for high-frequency thought snippets and distribution support, not core proof archive.

## Brand Consistency Rules (mandatory)
- Use only: `Vitan Architects`
- Never use in new public posts: `Jagrut & Partners`, `#jagrutandpartners`, `vitanarchitects.com` email/domain references.
- Canonical website: `https://www.vitan.in`
- Canonical inquiry contact in new outbound content: `connect@vitan.in`

## Conversion Hooks (required in every post)
- Include exactly one intent CTA:
  - "Book a 20-minute project-fit discussion"
  - "Request a site-responsive concept review"
  - "Share your project brief for a first-pass feasibility response"

## Weekly Measurement Pack
- Post-level: reach, saves, comments, inbound DMs, CTA clicks/replies.
- Funnel-level: touched, replied, meeting booked, proposal started, proposal won.
- Brand-hygiene: count of legacy-name/domain traces discovered and corrected.

## Kaizen Loop
1. Measure weekly content + funnel outputs.
2. Keep highest-performing pillar unchanged for one more cycle.
3. Replace lowest-performing pillar format with a new test variant.
4. Log one strategy change per week in memory with rationale.
