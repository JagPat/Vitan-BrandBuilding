# Speaking Proposal: ARCASIA Forum 23 (New Delhi/Jaipur 2026)

**Topic**: The Modern Vernacular: Climate-Responsive High-Density in Gujarat
**Speaker**: Ar. Jagrut Patel, Principal Architect, Vitan Architects
**Platform**: ARCASIA Forum 23 — "Bringing Back… Vernacular Wisdom"
**Date**: September 8-12, 2026
**Location**: New Delhi / Jaipur, India

---

## Abstract
As Asian cities face the dual pressures of unprecedented urbanization and climate volatility, the "International Style" of glass-and-steel monoliths is increasingly proving inadequate. The solution lies not in nostalgia, but in the intelligent translation of **Vernacular Wisdom** into modern, high-density typologies.

This presentation explores Vitan Architects’ "Contextual Modernism" through two landmark projects in Ahmedabad: **Parijaat Eclat** and **Navratna Skyscraper**. We examine how traditional Gujarati architectural principles—specifically the *Chowk* (courtyard) for passive ventilation and the *Jali* (perforated screen) for solar shading—are reimagined as high-performance facade systems and vertical social spaces.

**Key Learning Objectives:**
1. **Vertical Vernacular**: How to adapt traditional low-rise climatic strategies (ventilation shafts, shading fins) to 100m+ residential and commercial towers without compromising floor area efficiency.
2. **Material Memory**: Integrating locally-rooted textures and materials into large-scale institutional projects to maintain a sense of place in a globalized urban landscape.
3. **The ROI of Context**: Demonstrating how vernacular-inspired design reduces long-term operational costs (cooling loads) and increases project desirability for local residents who value climate-responsive comfort.
