# ArchDaily Submission Guidelines
**Reference**: VITA-211
**Date**: 2026-04-16

ArchDaily is the world's most visited architecture website. Submitting a project is a multi-step process that requires a comprehensive set of data and assets.

## 1. Required Metadata
- **Project Name**: Official name of the project.
- **Location**: City, Country (specific address is better).
- **Architects**: Main firm name (Vitan Architects).
- **Lead Architects**: Individual names of the lead architects.
- **Area**: Gross Built Area in square meters.
- **Project Year**: Completion year.
- **Photographs**: Photographer credits are mandatory.
- **Manufacturers**: List of main products and brands used (e.g., Saint-Gobain, Kohler).
- **Design Team**: Full list of contributors from the firm.
- **Clients**: Name of the client/developer.
- **Engineering**: Structural, MEP, etc.
- **Landscape**: Landscape architect/firm.
- **Consultants**: Lighting, acoustic, etc.

## 2. Text Requirements
- **Description**: Minimum 500 words is recommended for a "Featured" project.
- **Language**: English (mandatory). Additional languages (Spanish, Portuguese, Chinese) are optional but help with regional sites.
- **Focus**: Narratives should cover the context, the design challenge, the architectural solution, materials, and the impact on the user/environment.

## 3. Visual Assets
- **Photographs**: High-resolution (at least 2000px on the shortest side). JPG or PNG.
- **Drawings**: Mandatory for publication. 
  - Site Plan
  - Floor Plans
  - Sections
  - Elevations
  - Diagrams/Sketches (optional but highly recommended)
- **Format**: PDF, JPG, or PNG. High resolution, clear line weights. No watermark or heavy borders.

## 4. Submission Process
1. **Account**: Create a firm profile on ArchDaily.
2. **Platform**: Use the "Submit a Project" form on the ArchDaily platform.
3. **Review**: The ArchDaily editorial team reviews every submission. Not all projects are published.
4. **Publication**: If selected, the project is published on the main site and potentially regional editions.

## 5. Tips for Success
- **Hero Image**: Choose a striking exterior or interior shot as the primary thumbnail.
- **Storytelling**: Don't just list features; tell the story of why the project matters.
- **Completeness**: Submissions with complete metadata and technical drawings are much more likely to be featured.
