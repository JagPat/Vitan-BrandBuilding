# VITA-297 VALUE_LEAD Qualitative-First Normalization

Date: 2026-04-06 UTC
Issue: [VITA-297](/VITA/issues/VITA-297)

## What changed

- Updated `Business development/shared-workspace/references/sensitivity-protocol.md` so `VALUE_LEAD` no longer implies an always-on `10-15%` savings claim.
- The guardrail now defaults to qualitative value-first framing such as premium perception, decision confidence, execution clarity, and reduced downstream drift.
- Numeric savings or value-loss language is now explicitly allowed only when the draft or approval packet names a canonical, approved external-proof source.

## Why

- [VITA-296](/VITA/issues/VITA-296) had to remove unsourced outward-facing `10-15%` language to reach a send-ready standard.
- Leaving the old protocol wording in place would keep recreating the same review-loop and claim-risk ambiguity for future outreach work.

## Downstream effect

- Outreach drafts can still apply `VALUE_LEAD`, but they should do it through qualitative client value unless a stronger approved proof source is present.
- `NO_PRICE_FIRST` remains unchanged; price should still not lead the message.
- Reviewers and drafting agents now have a protocol-level rule they can cite directly when rejecting unsupported numeric value claims.
