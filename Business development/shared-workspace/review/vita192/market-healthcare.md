# Market Brief: Healthcare

Date: 2026-04-04
Issue: [VITA-192](/VITA/issues/VITA-192)

## Snapshot

- Strong institutional adjacency, but Vitan should enter selectively.
- The demand base is real and growing, yet the highest-value path is not a direct jump into large tertiary hospitals.
- The better first move is specialty clinics, diagnostics, day hospitals, and patient-facing healthcare environments where workflow clarity and experience design are visible.

## Market Size And Direction

- IBEF estimates India's hospital market at about USD 98.98 billion in 2023, projected to reach about USD 193.59 billion by 2032.
- Separate 2025 IBEF reporting cites roughly Rs. 2,61,900 crore (USD 30 billion) invested across healthcare and pharma between 2022 and 2024, with hospitals as a major investment segment.
- Government capacity expansion also continues: Cabinet-backed programs approved over Rs. 15,000 crore for additional MBBS and PG seats, while PM-ABHIM continues strengthening public health infrastructure through FY2025-26.
- This makes healthcare one of the clearest long-duration built-environment demand pools in India.

## Why Vitan Can Win

- Capability overlap is moderate, not high.
- The overlap exists where healthcare environments need calm user experience, clear circulation, human comfort, and stronger identity than generic institutional design.
- Vitan's brand philosophy fits outpatient, wellness, diagnostics, and specialty-care settings better than highly technical acute-care mega campuses on day one.

## Best Entry Segments

- Diagnostics and imaging centers.
- Day-care surgery and specialty clinics.
- Women's health, fertility, eye care, dental, oncology day-care, and rehabilitation environments.
- Premium hospital upgrade zones such as OPD, waiting, patient-room, family, and public interface spaces.

## Competitive Intensity

- High.
- Specialist healthcare architects, engineering-heavy firms, and hospital-consulting ecosystems already shape the large-project market.
- Vitan should avoid entering through the most compliance-heavy project types first.

## Entry Strategy

### Offer
- Sell "human-scaled healthcare environments" rather than trying to out-technical specialist hospital planners.
- Focus on patient flow, wayfinding, calm materials, family comfort, and brand trust in care spaces.

### Target account profile
- Ahmedabad specialty hospitals and diagnostics chains undergoing refresh or expansion.
- Premium clinics and day-hospital operators building multi-location networks.
- Developers or hospital promoters who need a stronger patient-facing architecture and interiors layer.

### First wedge
- OPD / diagnostics / day-care / specialty center planning and experience upgrade work.
- Partner where needed for technical healthcare planning rather than pretending full-stack hospital specialization from day one.

## Risks

- Compliance and MEP coordination can outrun current visible proof if the project scope gets too technical too quickly.
- Clinical workflow mistakes are high-consequence.
- Procurement and stakeholder stacks are more complex than commercial or residential work.

## Recommendation

- Enter selectively.
- Keep this as the third prioritized lane, after flex workspace and senior living.
