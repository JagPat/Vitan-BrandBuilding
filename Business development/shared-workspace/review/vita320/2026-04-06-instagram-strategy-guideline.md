## Decision Summary

Approve an Instagram system built around:

- `2` saveable carousels per week for authority, shares, and saves.
- `1` discovery reel per week for non-follower reach.
- Daily or near-daily Stories for light interaction, proof-of-work, and audience listening.
- A monthly theme rotation that keeps Vitan relevant to current architecture conversation without drifting into generic lifestyle content.

## Audience And Positioning

Primary Instagram audience for this guideline:

- architects
- builders
- real estate developers
- age band `30-50`
- Gujarat-first with India-wide credibility ambition

Position Vitan on Instagram as:

- premium but not flashy
- climate-aware and context-aware
- process-literate
- calm, specific, and proof-led

The feed should feel like a studio with built judgment, not a moodboard account and not a meme account.

## Current Platform Signal

### What the platform is rewarding now

- Reels remain the main discovery layer in India, and Instagram is testing a Reels-first mobile experience for some Indian users.
- Trial Reels now let creators test content with non-followers before sharing to followers, which is useful for risk-controlled experimentation.
- Carousel posts continue to overperform for engagement and saves versus single-image posts.
- Stories are better used for retention, polls, reminders, and "save this" utility sequences than for pure reach.

### Strategic implication for Vitan

- Use Reels to widen reach.
- Use carousels to build authority and save/share behavior.
- Use Stories to turn passive viewers into repeat viewers and DM responders.
- Do not overinvest in single still-image posts unless the image is exceptional or tied to a milestone.

## Overarching Brand Strategy For Instagram

### Content mix

- Project proof: `35%`
- Process and execution intelligence: `25%`
- Founder/design point of view: `20%`
- Materiality / detail / craft: `10%`
- Current architecture conversation and news reaction: `10%`

### Creative rules

- First slide or first 2 seconds must carry a concrete hook.
- Captions should explain what the audience can learn, not merely what Vitan did.
- Every post should resolve into one of these outcomes:
  - save this
  - share this with a partner/team member
  - DM us about a project problem
- Use people, movement, sketches, site process, and details to humanize premium work.
- Prefer real project evidence over abstract quote cards.

### Visual direction

- Warm, precise, material-led.
- Avoid over-edited renders as the default.
- Use sequences: drawing -> site -> detail -> built outcome.
- Keep branded overlays minimal and consistent.

## Weekly Instagram Template

### Tuesday: Authority carousel

- Format: `6-8` slides
- Goal: saves and shares
- Example hooks:
  - `What makes a home feel cooler before the AC is switched on`
  - `3 planning moves that reduce friction on premium residential projects`
  - `Why this detail matters more than the headline facade`

### Thursday: Discovery reel

- Format: `20-35` seconds
- Goal: non-follower reach
- Structure:
  - `0-3 sec`: visual hook
  - `3-20 sec`: one idea, sequence, or reveal
  - `20-35 sec`: calm CTA
- Best reel types:
  - sketch-to-site
  - material close-up to finished space
  - walkthrough with voiceover
  - founder POV on one current architecture topic

### Saturday: Proof post

- Format: single image or `3-5` slide micro-carousel
- Goal: profile visits and trust reinforcement
- Best use:
  - completed project snippet
  - client-relevant planning insight
  - detail shot with one sharp lesson

### Stories on non-feed days

- `3-5` frames max
- Use for:
  - polls
  - question stickers
  - site snippets
  - "save this checklist" reminders
  - reposting architecture-world developments with Vitan commentary

## Monthly Framework That Adapts To Trend Cycles

### Week 1: Climate and context

- Tie into the strong current interest in climate-responsive and environmentally responsible Indian residential architecture.
- Content types:
  - passive cooling carousel
  - material choices reel
  - site orientation story poll

### Week 2: Process and execution clarity

- Show how design decisions reduce ambiguity for builders and developers.
- Content types:
  - drawing-to-built carousel
  - coordination reel
  - "what gets decided early vs late" story sequence

### Week 3: Authority and proof

- Publish built work, recognitions, publication mentions, consultant coordination strength, or delivery rigor.
- Content types:
  - proof snapshot carousel
  - founder POV reel
  - detail-led proof post

### Week 4: Architecture-world relevance

- React to one current architecture topic, project, or development without becoming a news account.
- Good hooks right now:
  - climate-responsive homes
  - educational/public architecture visibility
  - design archives and legacy conversations
  - humane/ecology-led infrastructure

## Post Ideas

1. `7-slide` carousel: how one planning decision improved light, movement, and privacy.
2. `5-slide` carousel: material palette from drawing board to built edge condition.
3. Reel: `20-second` site walkthrough with one narrated insight.
4. Reel: founder answer to one question a developer often asks too late.
5. Carousel: three things Vitan checks before design language is frozen.
6. Story poll: `Which matters more here: daylight, privacy, or movement?`
7. Proof post: one image, one lesson, one CTA.

## Reel Concepts

### Reel concept 1: Sketch -> Site -> Space

- Show one idea progressing from linework to built form.
- Caption angle: design intent retained through execution.

### Reel concept 2: One detail, one outcome

- Start on a striking joinery/material/detail shot.
- Explain the user or climate outcome it creates.

### Reel concept 3: What developers miss

- Founder voiceover.
- One avoidable planning mistake.
- Calm, non-preachy finish.

### Reel concept 4: This week in architecture, translated for clients

- React to one current architecture development.
- Translate it into why it matters for decision-makers commissioning work.

## How To Increase Likes, Saves, Shares, And Followers

- Optimize for saves and shares first; likes will follow if the content is useful.
- Put a statement on slide 1, not just a pretty image.
- Use founder voice or founder face at least `2-4` times per month to strengthen memorability.
- Build recurring series names so people recognize Vitan formats quickly.
- Post at the current DPM hypothesis window of `19:00-21:00 IST` for Instagram, then refine after actual result data.
- Use Trial Reels once per month for riskier experiments before exposing them to followers.
- Treat comments and DMs as research inputs; questions should feed the next carousel or reel.
- Keep carousels practical enough that architects save them and developers forward them internally.
